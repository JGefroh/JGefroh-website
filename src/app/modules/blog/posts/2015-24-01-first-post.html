Another Attempt

My previous attempts at technical blogging using Wordpress and Jekyll have failed. I got so focused on the appearance and trying to make a cool looking blog that I never got around to actually writing posts.

This blog is different though - I'm not focused on making it pretty and I don't have to work with a whole CMS interface - just a plain .html file and a simple .json post registry.


The Idea

The blog is incredibly simple, and works in a two step process.

When the page first loads, it checks the URL after the #/blog/posts/ and loads the blog post if it is present.

To generate the post listing, I load a .json file that contains the list of posts that is shown to the user. When you click on a post heading, it re-requests the page with the new URL, starting the cycle over again.
