I ended up redoing my website sooner than I thought.

I liked my 2016 website, but I really wanted to focus on making it even more sleek and aesthetically pleasing. My teammate had redesigned her website recently, and that inspired me to do another redesign.

One major change I made was to ditch AngularJS and replace it with Jekyll. I had attempted to use Jekyll a couple years ago on my site, but removed it due to how long it took to change content. Now that I have more experience with how to structure the site to make it maintainable, and actually have a blog, Jekyll seemed like a perfect option for me.

I think it turned out well.
