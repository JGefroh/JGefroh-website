I showed my original website to one of my friends, proud of the work I done. After a mere glance he told me that it "looked old." What? That can't be! It must be a mistake! I quickly showed it to another friend, and she confirmed the bad news: my website was "Windows XP old." It was time to bring my website to the modern age.

The Overhaul

I sat down and thought to myself what requirements I had for the website overhaul. While some of the requirements were the same from my previous website, others had changed. The "No Javascript" philosophy was out - most people had Javascript enabled, and I figured it was time to join the club. I wanted to add a place for my resume and wanted a site that was easily updated.

In the end I came up with the following list of requirements:
  • A gallery for my projects
  • A modern appearance
  • A place to put my resume and skills
  • Easily updated with content
  • Simple and minimal
  • Rapid page loading times
  • Compatible with many browsers and window sizes

A modern appearance was easy enough. Everyone was using Bootstrap, so I figured I should learn how to use it as well.

The difficult part would have been the "easily updated" requirement. Footers and navigation bars appear on every page, and would have had to be changed manually.
Luckily, I had recently learned about a templating technology called Jekyll, which allowed me to split my website into multiple parts and combine them in layouts with a compiler. As a result, I only had to write my navigation bar and footer once. Even the slideshows and the link bar of this project page are merely components that were combined by Jekyll.

DRY at its finest!
